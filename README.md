# PhD Research: Ethereum-Based P2P Payments for MSMEs in Nairobi

## Overview
This repository contains the academic proposal and research framework for my PhD study at **Mount Kenya University**. The research focuses on the determinants of blockchain adoption (specifically Ethereum Layer 2s and Stablecoins) and its impact on financial inclusion among 460 Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) in Nairobi, Kenya.

## Research Context
Despite Kenya's leadership in mobile money (M-Pesa), MSMEs face high transaction costs and a lack of cross-border interoperability. This research utilizes the **UTAUT framework** and **PLS-SEM modeling** to empirically test how Ethereum-based programmable settlement can bridge these gaps.

## PhD Research Objectives
1. Examine the influence of performance expectancy and social influence on blockchain P2P adoption.
2. Evaluate the role of perceived regulatory risk in the Kenyan fintech environment.
3. Analyze the impact of Ethereum-based settlement on enterprise-level liquidity and credit accessibility.
